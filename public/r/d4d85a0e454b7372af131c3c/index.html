Conversion

Your Free plan's button asks people to "talk to sales"

Your homepage sells self-serve speed ("Paste your URL... in minutes", "Start for free"). But on the pricing page every plan button, including the $0 Free plan's "Get Free", links to /talk-to-sales instead of signup. Someone who clicks "Get Free" expecting to start on their own hits a sales-contact step instead. That is friction sitting on the exact zero-commitment entry point your homepage promises is frictionless.

Source: https://www.adkumo.com/pricing · Checked 2026-07-17 · Method: opened the pricing page and followed each plan button's link target
Messaging

"100 ads from one prompt" is hard to square with "15 image generations / month"

Your page title and hero promise "100 ads, on brand, from one prompt." Your pricing meters usage as "image generations" and caps the Free plan at 15/month and Starter at 120/month. A first-time visitor can't easily reconcile "100 ads from one prompt" with "15 generations a month," and that gap tends to create hesitation right at the pricing decision — the moment you least want confusion.

Source: adkumo.com + /pricing · Checked 2026-07-17 · Method: compared the public hero claim against the published plan limits
Trust

Your only social proof is launch-directory badges

The "As featured on" row shows launch-aggregator badges (Super Launch, Tiny Startups, and similar). There are no recognizable customers, no case studies, and no usage numbers on the page. For a product sold to marketers — people who judge marketing for a living — third-party signals that read as directory listings do less to lower the risk of signing up than a single named customer or a concrete result would.

Source: adkumo.com (homepage "As featured on" section) · Checked 2026-07-17 · Method: reviewed the public trust-signal row as a first-time visitor
